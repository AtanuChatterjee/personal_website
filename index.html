About

I am a soft-condensed matter physicist and the focus of my research lies at the intersection of complexity science and non-equilibrium thermodynamics. In my research, I try to understand, address, and quantify these observable features of complex systems through the fundamental laws of physics and statistical mechanics.

Education

I hold a Ph.D. in soft-condensed matter physics from Worcester Polytechnic Institute, where I studied pattern formation during thermal convection through infrared calorimetry in a Rayleigh-Benard system. I also hold a master's degree in computational science and applied mathematics from the Indian Institute of Technology Madras. During my time there, I worked on quantifying the emergent structure and predicting the spread of contagion in the large-scale bus transportation networks in major Indian cities.
